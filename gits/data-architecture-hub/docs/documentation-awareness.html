## Office word or ppt are more than a simple file
The conceptual structure of a slide-based presentation includes: * presentation - sequence of slides intended to be viewed by an audience. * slide - frame containing one or more pieces of text and/or graphics. * slide layout - organization of elements on a slide. * note - slide annotation, reminder, or piece of text intended for the presenter or the audience. * handout - printed set of slides that can be handed out to an audience for future reference. * comment - text attached to a location on a slide, visible while editing or reviewing a presentation, but not during a slideshow.

Exploring different approach

Step back: What do we need? What are our requirements? * Agility * Versioning & Retention * Split role and access * Format

## Agility requirement
* Ability to collaborate on content production easily (delta difference, merge easily, no latency on modifications propagation…) * Accelerate content publication and rendering

Format requirement

  • Presentation should accommodate for different populations (publish for different communities)
  • Possible to change the look & feel depending on the audience and AXA chart/templates
## Split role and access requirements
* Producers focus on the content * UX designer/Communication Specialist can work on the presentation * Consumers have access only to published document

Versioning and retention requirements

  • Content and presentation may have different lifecycle
  • Some publications may be only for communication purpose and deleted afterward
  • Some publications are due to remain for compliance or auditability purpose
## Solution design principles
Separate the content from the container ie how the content is published (html, word, pdf, ppt…) * Focus on the content and not the rendering during the production * Low latency and resource consumption (allowing to work on other applications in parallel) * Delta difference comparison * Publication in multiple formats (html, pdf, word, ppt) + automation when possible * Durability - make sure that the document can be still edited with various solutions in the future * Simplicity (ease of use, no training)

Reuse existing standard markup language

Based on our requirements, we have selected Markdown for its simplicity.

But for a more advanced documentation editing features, ReStructuredText, Asciidoc are better alternatives.

PROS: * Simple and easy to learn * No coding skills required * Human readable * Portability if no extensions

CONS: * Lack of formal specification * Lack of built in extensibility & lot of flavors

POC with reveal.js: * Challenging to manage images (resize multiple time) * Low maturity * Nice feature : navigation options (up down left right) * Require expertise in programming * Conclusion good but very limited and does fit the requirements

Key takeaways

  • Possbile industrial process with segregation of the view VS content

  • There are some possibilities for document publications to explore

  • This approach is relevant for references documents, user manuals, etc.

  • But for publications challenging to remove PPT, may not be the goal

  • SharePoint is multi-usage and still relevant even if it does not fit our requirements for content production

  • By design there is a trade-off between the 2 approaches

    • WYSIWYG approach is fast but difficult to maintain over time
    • Segregation of content VS presentation require additional maturity and effort (+tooling)
